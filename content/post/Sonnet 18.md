## Preface {#preface}

网上冲浪的时候，时常看到 _十四行诗_ 这样的字眼。
那时我还以为是某一首很出名的诗歌，现在才知道这是一种题材。
于是，我让 GPT 给我选了一首诗来学习——莎士比亚的 _Sonnet 18_.


### Early modern English {#early-modern-english}

需要注意的是莎士比亚使用的是早期的现代英语，和现代的英语出入较大。

| early | modern |    |
|-------|--------|----|
| thou  | you    |    |
| thee  | you    | 宾格 |
| thy   | your   |    |
| thine | yours  |    |
| art   | are    |    |
| hath  | has    |    |

### Phase conclusion {#phase-conclusion}

-   temperate: 温柔，克制
-   lease: 租约
-   eye of heaven: 太阳
-   complexion: 肤色
-   so long as = as long as: 更加庄严
-   all too... : 太过...。
-   brag: 夸耀


## Main {#main}

> Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?
>
> Thou art more lovely and more temperate:
>
> Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May.

夏天有时太过热烈（狂风摧残着五月的花蕾），反衬出了你的 **temperate**: 热烈但不猛烈，恰到好处。

> And summer's lease hath all too short a date;
>
> Sometime too hot the eye of haven shines,
>
> And often is his gold complexion dimm'd;

夏天却并不是永恒的，它的租约 (lease) 太过短暂。
即使是那么炽热的太阳（the eye of haven）, 也常常有暗淡的时刻。

> And every fair from fair sometime declines,
>
> By chance or nature's changing course untrimm'd;

所有美好事物的美都不可违抗自然规律，会在某个时刻衰退。

> But thy eternal summer shall not fade,
>
> Nor lose possession of that fair thou ow'st;
>
> Nor shall death brag thou wander'st in his shade,

前面说短暂的夏天、短暂的太阳，所有美都有一个终结的时刻。
这里却突然说，你的夏天是永恒的（你的美是永恒的）。
就连死神也不敢夸耀说你被笼罩在他的阴影之下。

> When in eternal lines to time thou grow'st:
>
> So long as men can breath or eyes can see,
>
> So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.

当你活在这永恒的诗句里，你的美就是永恒的，只要人们还活着，这首诗还存在。
有趣的是，四百多年过去了，我们今天仍然还在读这首诗。

最后让我们完整体会一下这首十四行诗。

> Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?
>
> Thou art more lovely and more temperate:
>
> Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May.
>
> And summer's lease hath all too short a date;
>
> Sometime too hot the eye of haven shines,
>
> And often is his gold complexion dimm'd;
>
> And every fair from fair sometime declines,
>
> By chance or nature's changing course untrimm'd;
>
> But thy eternal summer shall not fade,
>
> Nor lose possession of that fair thou ow'st;
>
> Nor shall death brag thou wander'st in his shade,
>
> When in eternal lines to time thou grow'st:
>
> So long as men can breathe or eyes can see,
>
> So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.
